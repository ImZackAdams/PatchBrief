Who it is for

Useful feedback should come from the teams closest to the weekly pain.

Best fit

Small IT teams, MSPs, and consultants

Especially teams that already track advisories manually, serve multiple clients, or need a plain-English summary for managers and client contacts.

  • Windows-heavy environments
  • Recurring client reporting
  • Product and vendor watchlists
Probably not

Teams that need full vulnerability operations

PatchBrief is not a scanner, patch manager, SIEM, or remediation tool. It is a decision-support brief for public advisory changes.

  • No environment scanning
  • No automatic remediation
  • No replacement for official advisories

What feedback matters

Three signals decide whether this should become a product.

Current workflow

How do you track public advisories today, and where does the process break down?

Useful output

Would a weekly source-linked brief be worth reading, forwarding, or paying for?

Missing must-haves

What source, format, or workflow would be required before this is useful?

Market feedback

Tell me if this would help your team.

A useful reply can be short: what you do today, whether this would save time, and what would make the idea a clear yes or a clear no. Negative feedback is welcome.
